Exploratory Data Analysis

Unit 2 of the ML module was dedicated to Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using Python, Jupyter Notebooks, and Google Colab. This module was instrumental in teaching me how to analyze data sets to summarize their main characteristics, often using visual methods. The hands-on experience with Python libraries like pandas, matplotlib, and seaborn was invaluable.

The practical aspect of this module was particularly enlightening. It allowed me to understand the importance of EDA in the data science workflow. I learned that before any machine learning algorithms can be applied, a thorough EDA is crucial to ensure the quality of the data and to gain insights that could guide further analysis. The interactive nature of Jupyter Notebooks and the collaborative features of Google Colab facilitated a deeper engagement with the data and fostered a collaborative learning environment.

Going forward, I plan to apply the EDA techniques learned in this module to my future projects, especially my thesis work. I will also continue to explore advanced visualization tools and techniques to enhance my data storytelling skills. The module has reinforced the notion that a good data analysis is as much about presenting the data effectively as it is about the technical computations.

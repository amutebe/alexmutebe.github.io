Exploratory Data Analysis

Unit 2 was dedicated to Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) which is instrumental in learning how to analyze datasets using visual methods.

The practical aspect of this module was particularly enlightening. It allowed me to understand the importance of EDA in the data science workflow. I learned that before any machine learning algorithms can be applied, a thorough EDA is crucial to ensure the quality of the data and to gain insights that could guide further analysis (Sahoo et al.,2019). The interactive nature of Jupyter Notebooks and the collaborative features of Google Colab facilitated a deeper engagement with the data and fostered a collaborative learning environment.

Collaborative Discussion 1: The 4th Industrial Revolution

Furthermore, I delved in UNIT 1 discussion, Sahr argued in his initial post that the fourth industrial revolution has catalyzed the emergence of the FinTech sector, which has significantly disrupted traditional financial services. I related his article to the transformative impact of mobile money service technology on Uganda’s banking industry, highlighting its role in providing financial services to remote areas with limited traditional banking infrastructure. I referenced the success of M-Pesa in Kenya as a pioneering example and noted the spread of such technologies across East Africa. I also addressed the issue of technological failures in banking, using TSB’s system malfunction as a case study. I concluded that machine learning techniques could be employed to predict and manage banking risks, particularly operational risks, and suggested that data science methods could help mitigate the frequency of software failures in the future.

References:

Sahoo, K., Samal, A.K., Pramanik, J. and Pani, S.K., 2019. Exploratory data analysis using Python. International Journal of Innovative Technology and Exploring Engineering, 8(12), pp.4727-4735.

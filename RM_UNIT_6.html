Quantitative Methods - Descriptive and Inferential Statistics

The exploration of Quantitative Methods was a significant highlight in Unit 6. It provided a comprehensive understanding of how numerical variables interact and can be analysed using statistical and graphical techniques. The unit also emphasized the importance of different levels of quantitative data, which are crucial in determining the appropriate statistical methods to use. The focus on measures of location and spread was particularly insightful. These measures, including mean, median, mode, range, variance, and standard deviation, are fundamental in summarizing data and understanding its central tendency and dispersion (Chakrabarty, 2021). This knowledge is invaluable in my data science journey, as it forms the basis for data analysis and interpretation.

Reference:

Chakrabarty, D., 2021. Measuremental Data: Seven Measures of Central Tendency. International Journal of Electronics, 8(1).

Validity and Generalisability in Research

we explored the differences between qualitative and quantitative data, and how each type can be analyzed and presented. We learned that quantitative data leads us into descriptive and inferential statistics, and that data cleansing and validation are crucial steps before analysis. We also delved into the concepts of reliability and validity, understanding that a measurement process needs to be reliable before its validity can be assessed. Initially, I felt a bit overwhelmed by the complexity of these concepts. However, as I progressed through the module, I began to appreciate the importance of these concepts in ensuring the quality and integrity of research. The practical examples and exercises were particularly helpful in understanding these concepts. However, I found some of the theoretical aspects challenging to grasp at first. The initial challenge in understanding these concepts could be due to their abstract nature. However, through additional reading and practice, I was able to overcome these challenges. This module has significantly enhanced my understanding of qualitative and quantitative data, and the concepts of validity and reliability. I’ve learned that careful consideration of these concepts is crucial in the design of research methods and the interpretation of research findings. In future research projects, I plan to apply these learnings. I will carefully consider the type of data I’m working with (qualitative or quantitative), and ensure the reliability and validity of my measurements. I will also continue to explore more advanced data analysis techniques and tools to further enhance my research skills.

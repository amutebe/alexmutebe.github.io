Validity and Generalisability in Research

Unit 9, we learned that quantitative data leads us into descriptive and inferential statistics, and that data cleansing and validation are crucial steps before analysis. During reliability and validity concepts, I understood that a measurement process needs to be reliable before its validity can be assessed (Cohen et al.,2017). Initially, I found these concepts confusing. However, as I progressed through the module, I began to appreciate the importance of these concepts in ensuring the quality and integrity of research. My summary post from unit 7 discussion, it underscores the importance of maintaining professional ethics, upholding research integrity, and considering the broader societal impact in all aspects of research and reporting.

Reference:

Cohen, L., Manion, L. and Morrison, K., 2017. Validity and reliability. In Research methods in education (pp. 245-284). Routledge.

Deep Learning for Automated Detection of Periportal Fibrosis

MSc project,

This project applied deep learning methods to improve the diagnosis of periportal fibrosis (PPF), a liver complication caused by Schistosoma mansoni infection. Traditional ultrasound diagnosis is highly operator‑dependent, so the study explored convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to automate detection and enhance accuracy. Using a dataset of 200 ultrasound images labelled with the Niamey protocol, images were anonymised, pre‑processed, and augmented before training a VGG16‑inspired CNN. The model achieved 80% accuracy, AUC of 0.89, and balanced sensitivity and specificity, demonstrating strong diagnostic potential even with low‑resolution inputs. The findings show that AI can support early fibrosis detection, standardise diagnosis, and improve accessibility in schistosomiasis‑endemic regions. Limitations included the small dataset and single‑reviewer labelling, but future work will expand datasets, incorporate fibrosis severity, and test alternative architectures.

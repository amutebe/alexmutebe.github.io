Collaborative Discussion 2: Legal and Ethical views on ANN applications

A summary Post

"The efficiency and creative advantages that GPT-3 offers today are astounding, ranging from enhanced business processes to personalized learning and creative writing (Hutson, 2021; Susskind, 2020; Luckin & Holmes, 2016). According to Hutson (2021), GPT-3, developed by OpenAI, is a cutting-edge AI writer with significant advantages. However, AI has some drawbacks. For example, biased or offensive information may be produced by AI because of biases in training data (Bender et al., 2021; Hutson, 2021). AI also raises ethical and reliability concerns. To mitigate risks related to AI biases, cautious data processing and preventing feedback loops in the echo chamber should be prioritized. The race for technological dominance and the competitive nature of AI development could make it more difficult for researchers to develop ethical AI (Cave et al., 2018). Due to the rivalry between tech companies, stakeholders may disregard ethical and safety standards, which could hinder the creation of appropriate AI legislation (Pereira et al., 2020; Rahaman et al., 2023). As AI evolves, ongoing research and collaboration among technologists, ethicists, and policymakers are crucial for responsible harnessing of its potential. It is imperative that governments and international organizations emphasize strict laws to safeguard AI users from unfavorable outcomes (Paterson et al., 2021; ACM, 2018). To prepare for changes brought about by AI, the public should keep learning, networking, and upgrading their skills (Jumaev, 2024). Both Sahr and Christopher offer a wide range of legal and ethical perspective of AI, reinforcing the need for adaptive learning and robust regulatory frameworks in the evolving AI landscape. In conclusion, constant focus should be paid to both benefits and challenges presented by AI, especially GPT-3 and now GPT-4o. Innovation in AI should be balanced with ethical and legal issues. "

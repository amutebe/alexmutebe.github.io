Introduction to Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

In the Introduction to Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), I learned about CNN architectures, including convolutional, pooling, and fully connected layers. A key focus was hyperparameter tuning, specifically adjusting the learning rate and number of epochs to optimize model performance.

Understanding CNNs is crucial for tasks involving image data, such as medical imaging and autonomous driving (Chauhan et al. 2018). Adjusting the learning rate taught me the balance between convergence speed and accuracy, while modifying the number of epochs helped me understand the trade-off between underfitting and overfitting. These exercises deepened my comprehension of training neural networks and finding the optimal balance between bias and variance.

Moving forward, I plan to utilize systematic hyperparameter Tuning using grid search and random search for efficient hyperparameter optimization. Advanced Optimization Techniques to explore algorithms like Adam and learning rate schedulers. Model Regularization by Implementing dropout, L2 regularization, and data augmentation to prevent overfitting.

  • Continuous Learning by staying updated with the latest developments in CNNs through journals, conferences, and online courses.

Training an Artificial Neural Network

In this unit, I learned about training Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) and the backpropagation algorithm. The course covered ANN architecture, the forward and backward propagation processes, and the significance of activation functions. Practical work involved experimenting with gradient descent cost function optimization based on Mayo's (2017) tutorial, where I adjusted iteration numbers and learning rates to observe their impact on cost reduction.

This module clarified the learning dynamics of neural networks and the critical role of hyperparameters. Through practical experiments, I discovered that:

  • High Learning Rates,cause the cost function to oscillate without convergence.Low Learning Rates, result in slow convergence, making the process inefficient. Iteration Numbers, must be balanced to ensure adequate learning without overfitting.
  • These insights highlighted the need for careful hyperparameter tuning to optimize training efficiency. The theoretical concepts from lectures were reinforced through hands-on application, emphasizing the iterative nature of model training and the importance of practical experimentation.

    • Going forward, I will, explore Advanced Optimizers such as Adam, to improve training efficiency. Use regularization techniques to mitigate overfitting and enhance model generalization. Systematize hyperparameter tuning by utilizing grid search and random search to automate and optimize the tuning process. Investigate activation functions to understand their effects on learning and performance.

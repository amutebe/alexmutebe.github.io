Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

In unit 2, we explored the significance of defining research questions and conducting literature reviews within a research proposal. Here are the key takeaways: The process of defining a research question is foundational. It shapes the entire study and ensures clarity. Despite its initial challenges, refining the research question is essential for successful research. A literature review involves surveying scholarly sources related to the chosen topic. It helps identify gaps, trends, and existing theories, allowing us to position our research within the broader context.

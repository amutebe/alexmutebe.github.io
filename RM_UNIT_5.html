Clustering

In unit 5, we explored different clustering algorithms such as K-means and hierarchical clustering. We also explored the evaluation metrics for clustering, including the Jaccard coefficient, which measures the similarity between finite sample sets and is a crucial tool in assessing the quality of clustering results (Sinaga & Yang, 2020).

Understanding different clustering techniques allows for more effective data exploration and pattern recognition, which are critical in many data-driven fields. One of the key assignment involved calculating the Jaccard coefficient, which provided hands-on experience in applying theoretical knowledge to practical problems. This task required us to implement the Jaccard similarity and interpret the results in the context of clustering performance (Niwattanakul et al.,2013).

Moving forward, the skills and knowledge acquired from this module will be instrumental in my future career as a data scientist. In practical applications, I will be able to leverage clustering techniques to solve complex problems in anomaly detection, and medical diagnosis research.

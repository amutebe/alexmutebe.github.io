Clustering

Cluster analysis, also known as clustering, is a fundamental technique specifically used for exploratory data analysis. The primary goal of clustering is to group a set of objects in such a way that objects within the same group (or cluster) are more similar to each other than to those in other clusters. During the module, we explored different clustering algorithms such as K-means, hierarchical clustering, and DBSCAN. We also explored the evaluation metrics for clustering, including the Jaccard coefficient, which measures the similarity between finite sample sets and is a crucial tool in assessing the quality of clustering results. One of the key assignments involved calculating the Jaccard coefficient, which provided hands-on experience in applying theoretical knowledge to practical problems. This task required us to implement the Jaccard similarity and interpret the results in the context of clustering performance.

  • GitHub

Understanding different clustering techniques allows for more effective data exploration and pattern recognition, which are critical in many data-driven fields. The ability to identify and group similar data points can lead to more informed decision-making and uncovering hidden patterns within the data. Learning about the Jaccard coefficient and other evaluation metrics was particularly insightful.

Moving forward, the skills and knowledge acquired from this module will be instrumental in my future career as a data scientist. In practical applications, I will be able to leverage clustering techniques to solve complex problems in anomaly detection, and medical diagnosis research.

Inferential Statistics and Hypothesis Testing

The module emphasized the importance of formulating clear hypotheses. Whether testing the mean difference between two groups or assessing associations, defining hypotheses correctly was crucial. I learned that a well-structured hypothesis guides the entire statistical analysis process. Descriptive statistics helped me summarize characteristics of the data, such as distribution, central tendency, and variability. For instance, in exercise 7.1, I observed that while Diet B was effective for some, its results were less consistent across individuals. In exercise 7.2, Comparing the results, Diet A has a higher mean weight loss (5.341 kg) than Diet B (3.710 kg), and a slightly smaller interquartile range (IQR), suggesting more consistent results among participants. While both diets were effective, Diet A appears to have been more effective on average and more consistent in its results across individuals. The ‘Other’ category is dominant in Area 1, suggesting a wider variety of brand preferences, while in Area 2, Brands A and B have a stronger hold, indicating a more concentrated preference for these brands among a larger sample of individuals.
